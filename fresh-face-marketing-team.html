Things nobody wants to hear but everyone needs to. These are the risks and realities that will determine whether this plan succeeds or fails.

1. You Can't Do This Alone

A solo esthetician working 6 days/week doing facials does not have the bandwidth to also run a Shopify store, write blog posts, manage social media, build email campaigns, run ads, and develop a product line. Something will break. You either hire help (virtual assistant, contractor, part-time marketer) or you reduce your service hours to free up business-building time. A common pattern: drop to 4 service days, use 2 days for business operations. You'll make less from services short-term but build the asset that scales.

2. Private Label is Harder Than It Looks

Minimum order quantities, FDA compliance, stability testing, liability insurance, packaging design, ingredient sourcing. A private-label skincare line can cost $15K–$50K to launch properly. The faster alternative: start as a curated retailer. Sell brands you already use in treatments (Dermalogica, etc.) through your Shopify store with wholesale margins (30–50%). Build the audience and revenue first. Then launch private label when you have the cash flow and customer data to do it right.

3. "Within an Hour of Myrtle Beach" Is Generous

An hour's drive from North Myrtle Beach covers Conway, Georgetown, Calabash NC, maybe Wilmington. The realistic service radius for a solo studio is 20–30 minutes — beyond that, clients choose someone closer. "Dominating within an hour" means dominating in search results and reputation, not necessarily expecting drive-time clients. Your Google rankings need to show up in those adjacent markets even if most clients come from 15 minutes away.

4. 7 Figures Takes 2–3 Years, Not 12 Months

Be skeptical of anyone who tells you otherwise. The brands that hit $1M in year one had either significant capital, an existing audience, or a viral moment. For a studio-to-product brand, the realistic timeline is: Year 1 builds the foundation and gets to $10K–$25K/month in products. Year 2 scales to $40K–$80K/month. Year 3 crosses the $1M annual mark. Trying to rush this leads to underfunded product launches, bad inventory decisions, and burnout.

5. Content is the Moat — And It's Daily Work

The competitors who will eventually challenge you aren't the ones with better facials. They're the ones who start creating content before you do. SEO compounds over time — a blog post published today ranks better in 6 months. An email list started today is 10x more valuable in a year. Every day you delay the website migration and content strategy is a day your competitors could fill the gap. The GlossGenius site is actively costing you customers every day it remains your primary web presence.

6. The Booking Tool Question

GlossGenius handles booking and payments well. Don't throw that away — just demote it. Embed the booking widget into your new Shopify site. Clients book through your site, GlossGenius processes it in the background. If GlossGenius doesn't support embedding, use a "Book Now" button that links to it. The key is: your brand's website is the front door. GlossGenius is the back office. Not the other way around.
