## What Most Skincare Menus Get Wrong

Here's what I see a lot on the Grand Strand: menus full of treatment names that sound impressive but don't tell you anything about how the treatment is customized for *you*. "Gold Facial." "Signature Glow Treatment." "VIP Luxury Experience." Okay, but... what are you actually doing to my skin?

A great esthetician Myrtle Beach or North Myrtle Beach should be able to answer that question in plain English. If she can't — or if she doesn't ask about your skin *before* starting — that's a red flag.

## The Green Flags: What to Actually Look For

**She looks at your skin first.** Before anything touches your face, a good esthetician does a thorough consultation. She's examining your pores, your texture, your hydration levels, any areas of sensitivity or concern. She's asking questions. She wants to know what you've tried before, what hasn't worked, what you're hoping to improve.

**She explains what she's doing and why.** You should never be lying there wondering what that product is or why she's doing that particular step. I talk through almost everything — not in a clinical lecture-y way, just "okay, I'm going to use this enzyme exfoliant because I can see your skin needs X." You leave knowing more about your skin than when you walked in.

**She's honest, even when it's not what you want to hear.** Some clients come in hoping a single facial will fix years of sun damage. I'd rather set realistic expectations than take your money and leave you disappointed. An esthetician who overpromises is not your ally.

**The space feels right.** You're about to close your eyes and let someone work on your face for an hour. You should feel completely at ease. At Fresh Face Studio, we're in a 1950s beach bungalow — it's cozy, it's quiet, it smells incredible, and there's exactly one client at a time. That's intentional.

## The Red Flags: When to Walk Away

- Cookie-cutter treatments with no skin assessment
- Feeling rushed or double-booked
- Pressure to buy products before you've even seen your results
- A therapist who barely speaks to you during the service
- No explanation of what's happening to your face

These things don't necessarily mean the esthetician is bad at her job — but they do mean the experience isn't built around *you*.
