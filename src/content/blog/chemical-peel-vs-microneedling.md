# Chemical Peel vs. Microneedling: Which Treatment Is Right for Your Skin?

Two of the most requested advanced treatments at Fresh Face Studio are chemical peels and microneedling. Both deliver real results — but they work in fundamentally different ways and treat different concerns. Here's an honest breakdown to help you decide which one makes sense for your skin right now.

## What a Chemical Peel Actually Does

A chemical peel uses acid solutions to dissolve the bonds between dead skin cells on the surface of your skin. The result: faster cell turnover, smoother texture, brighter tone, and reduced appearance of fine lines and sun spots.

At Fresh Face Studio, we use the Dermalogica Advanced Pro Peel system, which offers four different peel strengths. The right one for you depends on your skin type, sensitivity level, and what we're targeting.

**Best for:** Dullness, uneven skin tone, hyperpigmentation, sun damage, fine lines, mild texture issues.

**Downtime:** Minimal to moderate — expect some peeling for 3–5 days depending on peel strength.

## What Microneedling Actually Does

Microneedling creates controlled micro-injuries in the skin using fine needles. This triggers your body's natural wound-healing response, which produces new collagen and elastin. The result builds over weeks: firmer skin, reduced scarring, smoother texture.

**Best for:** Acne scarring, deeper wrinkles, melasma, age spots, loss of firmness, enlarged pores.

**Downtime:** 24–48 hours of redness, similar to a sunburn. Best results come from a series of 3–5 treatments spaced 4–6 weeks apart.

## The Honest Comparison

| | Chemical Peel | Microneedling |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary action** | Surface exfoliation | Collagen stimulation |
| **Depth** | Surface to mid-level | Mid to deep dermis |
| **Best concern** | Tone & brightness | Texture & scarring |
| **Results timeline** | Immediate glow, full results in 1–2 weeks | Gradual improvement over 4–12 weeks |
| **Series needed?** | Beneficial but optional | Yes — 3–5 sessions recommended |
| **Price at Fresh Face** | $130 | Starting at $250 |

## Which One Should You Start With?

If you've never had either treatment, start with a chemical peel. It's less intensive, more affordable, and gives you a clear sense of how your skin responds to professional treatments. Many clients start with a series of peels and move to microneedling when they're ready to address deeper concerns.

If you're already doing regular facials and peels and want to take your skin to the next level — especially for scarring, deep texture, or firmness — microneedling is the next step.
