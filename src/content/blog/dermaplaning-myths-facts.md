But first — let's dispel some myths.

## What Dermaplaning Actually Is

Dermaplaning is a manual exfoliation treatment where I use a single surgical-grade blade held at a precise angle to gently remove the outermost layer of dead skin cells along with vellus hair — what most people call peach fuzz. It's done with controlled, light strokes across the skin's surface. There's no electricity, no chemicals, no suction. Just a very skilled hand and the right tool.

I have to be licensed to perform this. The angle, the pressure, the technique — these aren't things you improvise. (And please don't buy a dupe razor from Amazon and try this at home. I'm begging you.)

## The Myths, Demolished

**MYTH: Your hair grows back thicker and darker.**

This is the one I hear the most, and I understand why — it's the same concern people have about shaving. But here's the thing: we're not talking about the same kind of hair.

Vellus hair (peach fuzz) is structurally different from terminal hair (eyebrows, leg hair, the hair on your head). The thickness and pigmentation of a hair is determined by the follicle — and dermaplaning does nothing to the follicle. It removes hair at the surface level only. When your peach fuzz grows back, it grows back exactly as it was: fine, soft, and colorless. I've been doing this for years. It does not grow back coarser. This is biology, not opinion.

**MYTH: It hurts.**

Almost every first-time client tenses up when they see the blade. By the end of the treatment, they're asking when they can come back. Dermaplaning feels like a credit card being lightly and smoothly swiped across your skin. There's zero pain involved when done correctly. Most people find it genuinely relaxing.

**MYTH: It's just shaving your face.**

This one frustrates me a little because it completely undersells the treatment. Shaving uses a multi-blade razor designed to cut dense, pigmented hair at or below the skin's surface. Dermaplaning uses a single surgical blade held at a specific angle to *exfoliate the skin* while simultaneously removing fine hair. The tool is different, the technique is different, and the result is completely different.

Shaving doesn't give you smoother skin texture. Dermaplaning does. Shaving doesn't improve product absorption. Dermaplaning does. Shaving doesn't give you an immediate, noticeable glow. I think you see where I'm going.

**MYTH: Everyone can and should do it.**

I appreciate the enthusiasm, but no — dermaplaning isn't for everyone. If you have active acne, I'm going to steer you toward a different treatment. Running a blade across inflamed breakouts spreads bacteria and creates micro-tears, which is the opposite of helpful. If your skin is very reactive or sensitive to anything that touches it, we'll talk through your options first.

A good esthetician will tell you honestly if a treatment isn't right for your skin. I'd rather redirect you to something that actually works than take your money for something that won't.

## Why I Love It (and Why Clients Do Too)

The results are immediate and they're noticeable. After a dermaplaning session near me at Fresh Face Studio, clients consistently comment on:

- **Skin texture** — instantly smoother, almost like silk
- **Glow** — that lit-from-within brightness you get when a layer of buildup is actually gone
- **Makeup application** — foundation goes on completely differently when there's no peach fuzz to catch it
- **Product absorption** — serums and moisturizers sink in faster and more effectively because the barrier of dead cells is cleared

It's also a zero-downtime treatment. You can go right back to your normal life. Just wear SPF (which you should be doing every day anyway).
