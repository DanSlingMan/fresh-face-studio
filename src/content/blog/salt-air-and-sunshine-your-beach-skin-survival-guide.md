Living here in North Myrtle Beach, I get to witness the most amazing transformations – and I'm not just talking about the facials! I'm talking about how our gorgeous coastal environment can either be your skin's best friend or its biggest challenge. Let me share some insider secrets for keeping your complexion glowing while you soak up all that South Carolina sunshine.

## The Salt Air Reality Check

Here's the thing about our beautiful beach air – it's loaded with salt, and while that ocean breeze feels incredible, it can be surprisingly drying. Salt literally pulls moisture from your skin, which is why you might notice your face feeling tight after a day at the shore. Don't panic! This is totally normal, and with the right approach, you can have your beach time and healthy skin too.

## Sun Protection That Actually Works

Let's talk SPF, honey. I know you've heard it a million times, but I'm going to say it again because I care about your gorgeous face: **broad-spectrum SPF 30 minimum, every single day**. Even when it's cloudy! Those UV rays bounce off the sand and water, creating a double-dose of exposure.

Here's my pro tip: apply sunscreen 20 minutes before heading out, and reapply every two hours. If you're swimming or sweating (hello, humid Carolina summers!), bump that up to every hour. I love mineral sunscreens with zinc oxide – they create a physical barrier and are perfect for sensitive skin.

## Your Post-Beach Skincare Ritual

This is where the magic happens! After your beach day, here's what I want you to do:

**Step 1:** Rinse off with lukewarm (not hot!) water to remove salt and sand without stripping your skin further.

**Step 2:** Use a gentle, hydrating cleanser. Skip anything with harsh scrubbing agents – your skin has been through enough for one day.

**Step 3:** While your skin is still damp, apply a nourishing moisturizer. This locks in hydration and helps repair your moisture barrier.

**Step 4:** Don't forget your neck, chest, and hands – these areas get forgotten but show sun damage first!

## Hydration is Everything

Salt and sun are a dehydrating duo, so you need to fight back from the inside out. Drink water like it's your job (because honestly, it kind of is for your skin's sake). I also recommend adding a hydrating serum with hyaluronic acid to your routine – it can hold up to 1,000 times its weight in water!
