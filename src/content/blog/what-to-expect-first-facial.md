I get it. Letting a stranger touch your face while you lie there with your eyes closed feels weird the first time. I've heard this from so many clients who almost talked themselves out of booking — and every single one of them said afterward, "Why did I wait so long?"

If you've never had a professional facial before, here's exactly what to expect. No surprises, no stress.

## Before You Come In

**What to wear:** Comfortable clothes you don't mind getting a tiny bit of product on. You'll be lying down for most of the appointment, so skip anything that feels constricting around the neck. You don't need to dress up — this is not that kind of vibe.

**Your face:** Try to arrive without heavy makeup if you can, but don't stress about it if your day didn't allow for that. I'll cleanse your skin thoroughly as part of the service either way. What I do ask: please don't exfoliate at home the day before. Give your skin a clean slate.

**Your timeline:** A Classic Facial is 60 minutes and a Custom Facial is 90. Build in a few extra minutes on either end so you're not rushing. The worst way to start a relaxing facial is to be frantically parallel parking.

## What Actually Happens, Step by Step

**Consultation.** Before I touch your skin, we talk. I'll ask about your concerns, your current routine, any sensitivities or reactions you've had, and what you're hoping to get out of today's appointment. Then I'll look at your skin closely — really closely, with good lighting — and tell you what I'm seeing. This part matters a lot. It's how the treatment gets customized for *you.*

**Cleansing.** We start with a thorough double cleanse to remove everything from your skin and create a clean canvas.

**Steam.** Gentle steam softens your skin and opens your pores. Most people love this part. It feels like standing in a warm shower, except you're lying down and someone is about to make your skin look amazing.

**Exfoliation.** Depending on your skin, this might be an enzyme exfoliant, a light acid, or a manual exfoliation. This step clears away dead skin cells and preps your skin to absorb everything that comes next.

**Extractions.** Okay, I know this is the one people are nervous about. TikTok has done a lot of damage here — those extraction videos are dramatic on purpose. In real life, at a professional facial, extractions are careful and controlled. I only extract what your skin is ready to release. There might be a brief moment of pressure, but it's not painful, and I'll always tell you what I'm about to do. Most clients are surprised by how manageable it is.

**Mask.** Chosen specifically for what your skin needs — hydration, calming, brightening, firming. This is the part where most people almost fall asleep. (The bungalow is very quiet. There's usually soft music. It's cozy.)

**Serums, moisturizer, and SPF.** The final layers go on while your skin is in the best possible state to absorb them. You'll feel the difference immediately.

## Does It Hurt?

No. The extractions can feel like a brief pinch of pressure if your pores are congested, but everything else — the cleansing, the steam, the mask, the serums — feels genuinely wonderful. People often tell me the hour went by too fast.

## After Your Facial

Your skin might look slightly pink for an hour or so, especially if you had extractions. This is completely normal and fades quickly. By that evening, most clients say their skin looks better than it has in months.

Try not to pile on full makeup for the rest of the day if you can avoid it — your skin just got a deep clean and fresh serums, and it deserves a few hours to breathe.

Stay out of direct sun for the rest of the day and wear SPF the next morning.

## How Often Should You Come Back?

Every 4–6 weeks is ideal — that's roughly aligned with your skin's natural cell turnover cycle. But honestly, even coming in quarterly makes a real difference. Consistency is the whole game in skincare.
